Look Out for Red Imported Fire Ants

Information for businesses and travellers to help keep Victoria fire ant free

Report RIFA in Victoria

What is red imported fire ant?

Red imported fire ants - RIFA (Solenopsis invicta) are an ant species from South America that has invaded South East Queensland.

Why are they a problem?

RIFA are a problem because of their poisionous stings, damage to the environment and impact on export and tourism industries.

How does this mean for me?

Businesses and travelers who are in RIFA infested areas need to exercise caution and diligence to not spread RIFA outside the outbreak area.

What do they look like?

RIFA are about 2 - 6mm long, reddish-brown with a dark abdomen. They are aggressive and cause painful stings. See the video for more details.

RIFA Outbreak in Australia

In Australia, RIFA were first detected in South East Queensland in 2001 and authorities have been attempting to contain and eradicate it since.

Most of the enviroment in Australia is suitable habit for RIFA. The economic costs of RIFA establishing in Australia are estimated in the billions of dollars.

Image source: The Guardian 2016.

Biosecurity

To prevent the spread of RIFA to other parts of Australia, any RIFA 'host material' leaving the outbreak zone needs to be RIFA free. Businesses transporting host material such as:

  • earth material (soil etc)
  • agricultural machinery/equipment
  • hay, mulch or straw
  • potted plants

to another state must ensure they obtain a certificate either from QLD government or as an accredited business. Businesses receiving host material must verify that the certificate is valid, inspect the consignment for RIFA and inform local authorities if any ants are found.
